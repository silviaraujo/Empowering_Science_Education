PREFACE

Scientific literacy and AI literacy are becoming inseparable pillars of contemporary education. Students today must learn not only how to ask scientific questions, evaluate evidence, and communicate findings, but also how to interact responsibly with AI systems that increasingly shape scientific and academic work.

This ebook was created to support that transformation. The project began as a collaborative initiative between the University of Minho and the University of Wroclaw to design a transparent, ethical, and student-autonomous model of Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL) enriched by multimodal scientific communication and grounded in responsible use of generative AI.

We asked ourselves:

  • How can teachers support authentic scientific inquiry without increasing workload?
  • How can students gain autonomy while maintaining academic integrity?
  • How can AI strengthen â€” not replace â€” scientific reasoning?
  • What does â€œgood use of AIâ€ look like in a rigorous academic context?

The result is a dual toolkit:

Teacher Toolkit

Designed to reduce workload while strengthening pedagogical structure. It provides:

  • a minimal-intervention workflow
  • an assessment system with 8 rubrics
  • guidelines for ethical AI use
  • a multimodality guide for scientific communication
  • templates for feedback and reflection

Student Toolkit

Designed to promote autonomy, scientific thinking, and responsible AI literacy. It guides students through:

  • asking rigorous research questions
  • searching and evaluating scientific evidence
  • producing multimodal outputs
  • communicating to expert and general audiences
  • reflecting, improving, and extending their inquiry

Both toolkits follow the same philosophy:
AI supports clarity and structure. Students remain the thinkers. Teachers guard integrity.

We hope this ebook will empower educators and learners to embrace an academically responsible, scientifically rigorous, and creatively multimodal approach to inquiry in the age of AI.

ğŸ§­ How to Use This Toolkit

This ebook is designed as a dual toolkit: one part for teachers, one part for students. The two workflows move in parallel, stay perfectly aligned, and support each otherâ€”without ever merging or replacing one another.

How Teachers Should Use It

  • Follow the Teacher Workflow to plan each stage of the inquiry project.
  • Use ready-made prompts, rubrics, planning templates, and ethical AI guidelines.
  • Consult the toolkit before each stage to understand your role, your decisions, and the checkpoints you must validate.

  • Follow the step-by-step Student Workflow from Stage 0 to Stage 3.
  • Use the prompts, checklists, and instructions at each step to organise your thinking, guide your research, and create multimodal outputs.
  • Refer to the ethical AI guidelines to use generative AI responsibly and transparently.
  • Work collaboratively and validate decisions as a group, especially in Stages 1 and 3.

How Both Parts Work Together

Although each toolkit can be used independently, the model achieves its full potential when both sides are activated:

  • Teachers design, validate, and ensure integrity.
  • Students investigate, analyse, create, and reflect.
  • AI provides structure, clarity, and scaffoldingâ€”but reasoning remains fully human.

When used together, the system creates a cohesive and rigorous inquiry environment that strengthens research literacy, multimodal communication, and ethical AI practices.
